Colombo Essentials

Galle Face Green
Galle Face Green, a lovely expanse of open space tucked between the busy Galle Boulevard and the Indian Ocean, is one of Colombo's most well-liked picnic spots.
Mount Lavinia
The nicest ocean view rooms at Mount Lavinia, probably Colombo's most popular heritage hotel, are virtually always reserved, unless you arrive early.
Gangaramaya Temple
The Gangaramaya Buddhist Temple, one of Colombo's most important places of worship, is just a short stroll from the picturesque Lake Beira.
Colombo Port Maritime Museum
The Colombo Port Maritime Museum, which is next to the Colombo Port, was constructed in 1676 inside a former Dutch prison. The museum, which has undergone one renovation, houses numerous significant historical artefacts, things, and data connected to Sri Lanka's ports.
Attidiya Bird Sanctuary
The Attidiya Bird Sanctuary, one of the most relaxing places to visit in Colombo, is home to over 50 different avian species as well as a variety of insects and butterfly species.
Paradise Road
Paradise Road is a charming little shop that Udayshanth Fernando opened in 1987. It sells high-quality contemporary clothes, the most of which is made in the area.
Beddagana Wetland Park
A very recent addition to the list of well-known locations in Colombo is Beddagana Wetland Park. In addition to improving the quality of life for the locals, the Sri Lankan government built it to safeguard the area from flooding.
Old Dutch Hospital
The Old Dutch Hospital, which dates back to the 17th century, is one of Colombo's oldest and best-preserved structures. It was constructed with the goal of provide medical assistance to Dutch East India Company employees.
Arugam Bay Beach
Arugam Bay Beach is the spot to go if you want to take a revitalising diversion away from the city and into the wilderness.
